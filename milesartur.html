User Profile: Artur Miles

Name: Artur Edmund Miles.

Date of Birth: April 29, 1783

Occupation: Advanced Particle Researcher and Chemist.

Location:Portland, Oregon.

Government Information: Artur was a man of moderate and balanced nature, with his mind focused on the safety and precision of experiments. Although he respected Hartmann's ambition, he approached projects with much more caution, often expressing concerns about the direction they were taking.

His loyalty to the government was unwavering. Especially after the explosion, the government faked his death so that, at his request, he could live a quiet life somewhere in South America. This change transformed him from a confident scientist into a solitary and reflective individual.

In the Nimbus project, Artur's role was to develop efficient methods for stabilizing and capturing the antineutrino, minimizing significant losses. His contributions were essential for the development of the first prototype. He was one of the first to join Hartmann's team and fought for him until the very end.

Death: No information about his death. After his move to the south, he became undetectable, and the witness protection program stopped providing updates about him after a few years of isolation.

Important Documents

Document: Last Letter.

Description: Artur wrote a last letter to the United States government after his move to South America. In it, he expressed his gratitude for the help he received and stated that he would pursue a different life, completely leaving science behind and living a simple life. He also mentioned that some members of the Consortium contacted him occasionally, but he remained loyal to the government and was willing to pass on any information about them. After that, he never wrote again.

Document not available in the records. Reason: Destroyed.
