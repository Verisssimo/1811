User Profile: Dr. Hartmann

Name: Victor Preuss Hartmann.

Date of Birth: October 11, 1775

Occupation: Lead Researcher.

Location: Eugene, Oregon.

Government Information: Hartmann was certainly a man ahead of his time. He was known for being ambitious and relentless, always getting what he wanted. For him, science was simple and a true means of power and transformation; his work was the great passion of his life. To him, the ends justified the means, so he did not hesitate to take risks in dangerous and complex experiments.

"Science must be pushed beyond the wrongly established ethical and moral limits," he would often say. This attitude often prevented him from gaining the recognition he deserved for his major scientific discoveries. Therefore, he began to work secretly for the government to make his life truly meaningful. He was the 1st scientist to work side by side with the government in such a significant way.

Hartmann faced resistance from a secret society that, after discovering leaks about the project, aimed to sabotage it and steal significant amounts of data, all while claiming that the government's ethics behind the experiments were, at the very least, questionable. Hartmann believed that the Consortium was the true obstacle to science.

Important Documents
